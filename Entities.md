### Governance and leadership

**President:** Count Etienne Davignon — former Vice-President of the European Commission; former President of Société Générale de Belgique; **Chairman of the Bilderberg Steering Committee 2000–2012**. His presence at the head of FoE ties it directly to the Bilderberg network.

**Founder:** Giles Merritt — former FT Brussels correspondent (1972–1983); columnist for the International Herald Tribune (1984–2010). In 2010 the FT named him one of its 30 most influential "Eurostars", placing him alongside the European Commission President and **NATO's Secretary General**.

**Board of Trustees (selected):**
- **Carl Bildt** — Co-Chair of ECFR; former Swedish PM and Foreign Minister; strongly Atlanticist
- **Joaquín Almunia** — former VP European Commission; ECFR Council member
- **László Andor** — former EU Commissioner; post-mandate RAND Europe engagement
- **Dacian Cioloș** — former EP leader (Renew), former Commissioner, former Romanian PM
- **Xavier Bettel** — current Luxembourg Deputy PM and Foreign Minister

### Staff — NATO and Atlantic Council pipeline

The Peace, Security & Defence programme is staffed almost entirely by NATO and Atlantic Council alumni:

| Name | Role at FoE | Previous affiliation |
|---|---|---|
| Thomas Van Vynckt | Head of Peace, Security & Defence | **NATO HQ Brussels** (Political Affairs & Security Policy + Operations divisions); **GLOBSEC** |
| Gábor Iklódy | Senior Fellow | **NATO Assistant Secretary General** for Emerging Security Challenges (cybersecurity, terrorism, WMD, energy security, strategic foresight) |
| Myroslava Gongadze | Senior Fellow | **Atlantic Council Nonresident Senior Fellow** (simultaneous); former **Voice of America** Eastern Europe bureau chief |
| Leila Alieva | Senior Fellow | Former fellow at **NATO Defence College**; Harvard, UC Berkeley, Kennan Institute |
| Mary Fitzgerald | Researcher | Works with **US Institute of Peace (USIP)** and ECFR |

### Editorial posture

Recent homepage content included *"Rest in peace the Atlantic Alliance: created by America's Greatest Generation but undone by one of its worst"* — a lament for US/NATO leadership decline authored by **Jamie Shea**, NATO's spokesperson and Director of Policy Planning for 27 years. The Ukraine Initiative, launched 2023, is FoE's largest current programme by visibility.

### Funding

No direct US government funding declared. Corporate membership model — companies pay for access to debates and the commissioner network. EU Transparency Register entry carries declared income breakdown, but the financials tab of their governance page is JS-rendered and was not accessible for direct scraping. The absence of US government funding distinguishes FoE from IRI/NED-linked organisations; its Atlanticism is structural (governance, staff, editorial) rather than funded.

### Assessment

FoE is best understood as a **Brussels-based transatlantic establishment network** rather than an explicitly Atlanticist think tank. Its Atlanticism is structural: the Bilderberg-connected presidency (Davignon), the ECFR-connected board (Bildt), the NATO-pipeline staff (Van Vynckt, Iklódy, Gongadze), and the editorial orientation toward NATO/Ukraine issues. It functions as a **post-mandate landing spot and civic engagement platform** for former commissioners across the political spectrum — its Board of Trustees reads as a who's who of retiring EU commissioners — giving it influence over the next generation of EU leadership via its European Young Leaders (EYL40) programme.

It is not a lobby group in the formal sense, but it operates in the space between think tank, alumni network, and transatlantic convening platform.

## Wilfried Martens Centre

**Type:** Think tank (political foundation)  
**HQ:** Brussels, Belgium  
**Founded:** 2007  
**Classification:** Atlanticist — | NATO-adjacent — | US-linked —

**Commissioner connections (declared):** VdL II: Andrius Kubilius (various roles), Henna Virkkunen (Board of Directors)

**Key facts:** Official think tank of the European People's Party (EPP), funded primarily by the European Parliament under the Regulation on European political foundations. EPP commissioners' connections to it reflect factional alignment within the Commission, not transatlantic network membership.
